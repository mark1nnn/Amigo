How to prepare materials for a website project?

Well-organised materials save time and reduce confusion. You do not need perfect copy before the project starts, but you do need enough input to shape the offer, the structure and the contact flow.

June 9, 20264 min read

What is worth collecting first?

  • a list of services or products,
  • a short company introduction,
  • current contact details,
  • logo files and any existing brand assets,
  • photos or notes about visuals you need.

You do not need finished copy

Rough notes are often enough to begin. Service descriptions, common client questions and short explanations of your process already give the project a useful foundation.

Examples are more helpful than abstract feedback

Links to websites you like, competitor references and a few comments about what feels right or wrong help define direction much faster than general adjectives alone.

Good materials also support SEO and contact

The better your offer is described, the easier it becomes to prepare headings, metadata and a sensible contact form. Review our services, browse the portfolio and reach out through contact if you want help reviewing what you already have.
